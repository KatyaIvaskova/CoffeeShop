Explore The World Of Coffee

by admin / 04th September, 2021

Coffee is a major commodity in the world of trade. In fact, it has been said that coffee accounts for over 15% of all global exports! Countries such as Colombia and Brazil are well-known for their quality beans, while others may be lesser known for this product. Many countries have developed their own signature style when it comes to brewing coffee. Italy is well-known around the world for its espresso drinks, which can often contain cream or milk. France tends to prefer lighter roasts with a higher caffeine content which the people enjoy from cafes on street corners throughout Paris and other regions in the country. In South America, most people tend to drink coffee brewed through a percolator or drip method instead of espresso. The same goes for Central America, where the traditional coffee brewing method is to use a drip filter or French press machine to create their beverage.

Freezing Coffee Beans - Why and How You Should Do It?

by admin / 14th October, 2021

Freezing coffee beans for storage has been a controversial topic in the coffee industry in the past but lately freezing beans have been seen as a good idea. Why is freezing coffee beans good idea and how should you do the freezing? Does freezing the coffee beans really make your coffee taste even better? Freshness is critical to a quality cup of coffee. Experts agree that coffee should be consumed as quickly as possible after it is roasted, especially once the original packaging seal has been broken. While there are different views on whether or not coffee should be frozen or refrigerated, the main consideration is that coffee absorbs moisture â€“ and odors, and tastes â€“ from the air around it, since it is hygroscopic (bonus vocabulary word for all the coffee geeks out there). Most home storage containers still let in small amounts of oxygen, which is why food stored a long time in the freezer can suffer freezer burn. Therefore, if you do refrigerate or freeze your beans, be sure to use a truly airtight container. If you choose to freeze your coffee, quickly remove as much as you need for no more than a week at a time, and return the rest to the freezer before any condensation forms on the frozen coffee. Freezing your beans does not not change the basic brewing process.

Heat Your Mug Before Making the Coffee

by admin / 09th November, 2021

Pre-heating your cup can be a great way to decrease the rate at which the coffee cools down. The best way to execute that is to heat some water and pour it into the cup. The higher the water temperature, the better the results. This will induce heat to the entire cup, making it ready to preserve the heat of the coffee that is to be poured in afterward. While this does not sound like much, it does slow down the rate of heat loss, rather than letting the coffee cool off rapidly. Do not forget to dry the cup after the water has done its task, as you definitely do not want to dilute your coffee. Using this technique of pre-heating the cup before pouring coffee in or simply brewing it inside is a great practice. In certain situations when, say, you are trekking through a forest or hiking on a mountain, not all forms of heating devices may be available to you. This preheating method can be applied in such situations, without the need for any electrical appliances, which is greatly beneficial for travelers on long tours. Make sure you do not empty the container immediately after filling it with hot water. This is because the mug takes a little time to absorb heat from the water and might not fully heat up in case the water is not kept in it for long.
